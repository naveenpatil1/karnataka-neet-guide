📝 Karnataka Domicile NEET Strategy Guide

Continuous LKG to 12th Standard Bangalore Schooling Baseline

📋 NEET Exam Structure & Domicile Rules

  • Total Questions: 180 Mandatory Questions (45 Physics, 45 Chemistry, 90 Biology)
  • Total Marks: 720 Marks (+4 for correct, -1 for incorrect, 0 for unattempted)
  • OMR Shading Buffer: 20 Minutes reserved out of the total examination window
  • State Quota Eligibility: 100% verified under KEA Clause A (Requires minimum 7 years of continuous/non-continuous study in Karnataka, finishing 10th or 12th from the state). Gives exclusive access to 85% state quota seats.
  • Karnataka capacity (2024-25): ~12,395 MBBS seats across 71 colleges; of these ~5,995 seats are in 35 private colleges (source: hellomentor KEA seat matrix).

📈 What the real closing ranks tell us (key insight)

The 2025 GM State-Quota ranks reveal the true targets — and they refine the tier bands:

  • Under ~4,000 → the top govt seats: BMCRI (1,299), Mysore (4,053).
  • ~5,000–12,000 → strong govt + best private G-Quota: MS Ramaiah (6,905), KIMS Hubli (8,343), MIMS Mandya (11,478), Kempegowda (11,641).
  • ~15,000–18,000 → most district govt colleges: VIMS Ballari (15,845), BIMS Belagavi (16,260), Hassan (16,221), Vydehi (16,640), SIMS Shivamogga (16,967), GIMS Gulbarga (17,229).
  • ~24,000–31,000 → the easiest govt/private G-Quota: SDM Dharwad (24,206), JJM Davangere (24,255), RIMS Raichur (27,935), Dr. B.R. Ambedkar (31,037).

Practical reading: a State-Quota rank under ~16,000 opens almost every government college in Karnataka; under ~8,000 reaches the top-tier ones (BMCRI/Mysore/KIMS Hubli). That rank is driven directly by the NEET score — which is what the test-precision work is building toward.

🏛️ Reputed colleges NOT in this table — and why

Several top Karnataka colleges are Deemed Universities — no state G-Quota; admission is all-India via MCC deemed counselling at management / NRI fees only (~₹18–30 L+/yr). Lower priority for an affordable seat:

  • JSS Mysuru (JSS AHER) · J.N. Belagavi (KAHER) · Sri Devaraj Urs, Kolar · KMC Manipal & Mangalore (MAHE) · BLDE Vijayapura · Yenepoya · Adichunchanagiri · BGS Global

Karnataka has ~71 MBBS colleges / ~12,395 seats (≈5,995 in 35 private); this table is a state-quota shortlist. More district govt institutes (Bidar, Koppal, Chamarajanagar, Kodagu, Chikkamagaluru, Karwar, Haveri, Yadgir, Chikkaballapura, Gadag…) exist at the ₹64,350/yr fee — add as needed.

💰 The ₹50-Lakh Insight (why score > everything)

Over a ~5.5-year MBBS, the same degree costs roughly:

  • G-Quota: ₹3.5–8.5 lakh total (Govt ₹64,350/yr · Private G-Quota ~₹1.54L/yr)
  • P-Quota: ₹63–69 lakh total (~₹11.5–12.5L/yr)

≈ ₹50–60 lakh difference. The marks a student gains by fixing precision & negative-marking discipline move them down the rank list — from a P-Quota fee into a G-Quota seat at the same college. Lower rank = same degree, ~₹50 lakh cheaper.

📜 Critical Post-Counselling Rules

The 1-Year Compulsory State Rural Bond: All candidates graduating under a Government Quota seat (G-Quota) across both pure government and private medical colleges are legally bound to complete a 1-year mandatory rural medical service assignment designated by the Government of Karnataka post-graduation, or pay a financial penalty.
